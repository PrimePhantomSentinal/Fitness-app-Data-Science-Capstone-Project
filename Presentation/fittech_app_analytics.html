The Funnel Reality

Baseline app utility is strong, proven by the 75% workout completion rate. Users log in and successfully finish their routines.

However, the UX falters at proactive engagement. Only 36.5% of users respond to push notifications, severely throttling the top-of-funnel flow toward premium subscription purchases (13.8%).

Strategic Mandate: The 13.8% purchase rate is the key business opportunity. ML is used to predict high-engagement users and guide personalized interventions.

Predictive Modeling (ML) Approach

Using Data to Predict the Future

We apply baseline Machine Learning not to perform magic, but to achieve two very specific, measurable business goals:

  • 1. Classification (Targeting) Identify users highly likely to stay engaged, allowing us to focus retention and reward budgets accurately.
  • 2. Regression (Valuation) Estimate a user's total session time quantitatively to forecast lifetime value (LTV).

Data Pipeline & Preparation

Target definition: "High Engagement" = Total session time > median.
Dataset: 600 users, balanced.

1. Split: 80% Train / 20% Test
2. Scale: StandardScaler applied to normalize numerical variance.
3. Encode: OneHotEncoder for categorical features (Gender, Region, Subscription, Goal, Workout Type).

ML Classification Models Evaluated

Prediction target: High_Engagement_Flag, where users with Total Session Time ≥ 5,943 minutes are marked as high engagement. Hover cards for actual metrics and model meaning.

Business Recommended Model

Logistic Regression

Chosen as the business winner because it balances performance with clear explainability for stakeholders.

Why It Wins for Business

Accuracy: 72.5%  |  High-engagement recall: 73%

Correctly identifies 44 of 60 truly high-engagement users and 43 of 60 truly low-engagement users, with 17 false alarms and 16 missed high-engagement users.

Reason to choose this model: Its coefficients are directly explainable, so the business can defend exactly which behaviors raise or lower high-engagement probability, not just receive a prediction.

Important inputs: total workouts dominates by a wide margin, followed distantly by Premium subscription; purchase rate, completion rate, age group, and goal type each contribute much smaller effects.

KNN Classifier

Actual Metrics

Reason not chosen: KNN has no coefficients to explain — it classifies by comparing each user to similar users, so the business can see that it predicted something, but not why.

ML Drivers: What Impacts Engagement?

The Real Driver: Workout Frequency

We selected Logistic Regression not just for its 72.5% accuracy, but for its perfect business explainability.

The model's coefficients show raw workout frequency dwarfs every other factor — its effect is over 3x the next-largest driver, Premium subscription. Once workout frequency is in the model, Completion Rate and Purchase Rate actually carry small negative coefficients, meaning their simple correlation with engagement is already explained by how often a user works out.

Strategic Mandate: Drive workout frequency and Premium conversion first — together they are the two strongest mathematical predictors of high engagement, far ahead of notification or completion-rate effects.
